1982/12/04: 1982 Constitution

On December 4, 1982, the Fifth Session of the Fifth National People's Congress reviewed and approved the historic 1982 Constitution, which also became the fourth constitution of the People's Republic of China. The 1982 Constitution included many contents and provisions of constitutionalism, laying the foundation for reform and opening up. The following bullet points summarize the contribution of the 1982 Constitution.

  • It removed language with Culture Revolution characteristics, such as "continue the revolution under the dictatorship of the proletariat".
  • The clause "The country is led by the Communist Party of China" is abolished, and the word "Communist Party of China" does not appear directly in the text of the Constitution.
  • It stipulates that "no organization or individual shall have privileges beyond the constitution and laws", including the Communist Party of China.
  • The relationship between the party and the law has been straightened. The separation of the party and government has been reinforced. The legislative power, judicial power and executive power have been clearly distinguished.
  • The positions of the President and Vice-President of the People's Republic of China were restored. The term of office is five years, and the re-election shall not exceed two terms.
