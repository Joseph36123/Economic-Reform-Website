2012/11/08: The Eighteenth National Congress of the Communist Party of China

In November 2012, the 18th National Congress of the Communist Party of China was held, and Xi Jinping was elected general secretary and chairman of the Military Commission to come to power. Subsequently, the Chinese Communist Party expanded its control over state-owned and private enterprises, leading at least 288 companies to amend their articles of association, reflecting the party's new corporate management policies.
